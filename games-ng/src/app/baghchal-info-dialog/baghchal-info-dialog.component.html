Bagh-chal

Bagh-chal, also known as "Tigers and Goats", is an asymmetric two-player board game from Nepal. One player controls the four Tigers, while the other player controls up to twenty Goats.

The Tigers start in the four corners of the board. On their turn, the player can move a Tiger to an empty adjacent space (connected by a line), or jump over an adjacent Goat to an empty space, thereby capturing the Goat.

The Tigers' goal is to capture 5 Goats by jumping over them.

The Goats start with no pieces on the board. On each turn for the first 20 turns, the player must place a new Goat in an empty space on the board. After all 20 Goats have been placed, the player must instead move a piece on each turn to an empty adjacent space (connected by a line).

The Goats' goal is to trap all four Tigers, so that they have no available moves.

On your turn, select the space(s) where you wish to move/place your piece, then click the "Play" button. Select (or deselect) spaces by clicking on them.
